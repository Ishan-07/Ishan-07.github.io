Gandhi on machines

My spinning wheel or even this toothpick, for that matter, is a machine. I hate not the machines, but this growing passion for machines. I hate the passion for the machines which work upon diminishing man power. Some talk about machine which could spare man power when thousands of people are thrown jobless on the streets. Yes, I want the human toil and time to be spared not just for a sect of people but for the humanity. I want the wealth to be accumulated not just in few hands but for all the people in the world. Today machines favor putting handful of people on top thousands.’

Mahatma and machines: Understanding Gandhi's thoughts on modern technology

Father of our nation, Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi is known for many things. From his thoughts on caste, creed and religion to his teachings of ahimsa and communal harmony, the Mahatma was a man who crafted the future of our country with... the power of his ideas. However, despite his ability to accept contemporary ways of living without distancing himself from the teachings of the past, the Mahatma was somebody who till the very end remained wary of what the modern way of life could bring with itself in the future. And the biggest proof of this is his oft-cited distrust of technology and the impact it could have on human life. Throughout his life, Gandhiji remained sceptical about the role modern technology could play in our lives and did his bit in persuading people to steer clear of the industrial revolution that was sweeping over the western world and had slowly started to take root in India too. And in his attempts to stop the industrialisation of India along the lines of what was being seen in the West, Gandhiji also became a vocal critic of the growing use of machines for increasing manufacturing output.

Mahatma Gandhi
2 October,1869-30 January, 1948

Mahatma Gandhi is very famous in India as “Bapu” or “Rastrapita”. The full name of him is Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi. He was a great freedom fighter who led India as a leader of the nationalism against British rule. He was born on 2nd of October in 1869 in Porbandar, Gujarat, India. He died on 30th of January in 1948. M.K. Gandhi was assassinated by the Hindu activist, Nathuram Godse, who was hanged later as a punishment by the government of India. He has been given another name by the Rabindranath Tagore as “Martyr of the Nation” since 1948.
